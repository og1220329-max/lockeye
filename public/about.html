Why LockEye exists

Not a motivator.
A friend who forces the issue.

LockEye is not here to hype you up. It does not send cheerful reminders or pretend a streak emoji will change your morning. It works the way a blunt friend works: it does not ask how you feel about the gym, it just gets you there, because the alternative costs you real money.

You already pay for a membership you barely use. That money is gone whether you show up or not. LockEye does not add a new expense, it redirects the one you already have. Show up and the app costs you nothing. Skip, and the stake you set moves to a charity you picked, not into LockEye's pocket beyond the small share that keeps it running.

"The result is simple. The friend who never lets you off the hook also happens to be the friend saving you money every month."

The psychology behind this is decades old: people are more motivated by avoiding a loss than by gaining an equivalent reward. LockEye just applies it where you've needed it most — your relationship with the gym you're already paying for.

The business model

We only make money when you skip. So we're incentivised to make you not skip.

LockEye's 15% fee only applies when a session is forfeited. When you show up and verify, the app earns nothing. That alignment isn't an accident — it means the app's entire business model is built on reducing the thing that earns it revenue. We win when you win.
